Music

Symphonic Band, Jazz Band, Indie Rock Band, Musical Pit Band... Name it all! My favourite source of joy is playing music with others and performing in front of an audience. The pure sense of euphoria that comes after every rehearsal and show is unmatched. I have a long list of stories to tell from my musical experiences, and I'd like to share a few highlights here.

My Journey

I could never be the musician I am without my high school Jazz Band. Here is where I learned true grit and dedication. I started off as a beginner drummer, along with another senior year drummer who had professional level experience. I was more than intimidated and always felt that I didn't deserve the spot, but while I was thinking all that, being a drummer was all I ever wanted. I worked harder than I ever knew I could before, and with that, I got better and better until I won multiple awards throughout my high school years. The most memorable award was the WBDI (Women Band Directors International) Award, given to the most outstanding musician in the Calgary Honours Girls in Jazz Band. Jazz drumming helped me experience the beauty of consistency and hard work. No matter how skill-less I was, hard work always paid off.

After such a memorable musical journey in high school, I entered a new musical phase in my life coming to Toronto for my university education. I was fortunate enough to meet friends who shared the same mmusic taste as I did, with the same level of passion, dedication, and skills. We formed an indie rock band called Orion's Belt, and we played gigs around Toronto. I got to experience the most exciting year, with so many new experiences including recording in a professional studio, playing live gigs to a local audience, and also getting to meet so many talented artists. The Toronto local Band scene is truly an amazing community. It is full of talented, passionate, and creative people, and through them, my appreciation for art as a whole grew so much. The music scene in Toronto wasn't just about music. It was a combination of culture, art, fashion, and lifestyle. I was so lucky to be part of it. Performing and creating music in a band allowed me to experience the joy of collaboration and the thrill of working with like-minded people with a shared goal, and my appreciation for art.

My musical journey continues, and my new station I am currently exploring is playing in musical pit bands. University of Toronto has an amazing theatre program, and I was lucky enough to be part of the pit band for two musicals: "FootLoose" and "The Guy Who Didn't Like Musicals". Playing in a pit band is a whole new experience compared to playing in a regular band. You have to be extremely precise and to do that it requires a lot of practice. But honestly, I love every bit of it. I've been a casual musical theatre fan for a long time, and it's been a long dream of mine to be part of a musical production. So not only am I fullfilling my dreams, I get to work with so many super talented musicians. I don't even know how to explain the feeling of euphoria after every rehearsal! I am just so grateful that I get to play music with such talented people.
